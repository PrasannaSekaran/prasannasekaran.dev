What Drives Me

I don't just build AI.
I build AI that acts.

AI Agents That Change Things
My deepest conviction: the most powerful software of our era won't be written by humans — it'll be orchestrated by AI agents that plan, reason, retrieve, and execute autonomously. I wake up thinking about how to build systems like that. Not demos. Not proofs-of-concept. Production agents that make a tangible difference in how people invest, decide, and operate at scale.
01
LLMs as Reasoning Engines
LLMs aren't chatbots to me — they're reasoning engines. I'm obsessed with prompting them at their limits: structured outputs, tool calling, chain-of-thought, multi-hop retrieval. Getting an LLM to consistently produce trustworthy, grounded decisions is the hardest and most rewarding engineering challenge I know.
02
Finance as the Ultimate Test Bed
Finance is unforgiving data. Noisy signals, adversarial markets, lagging information — if your AI works here, it works anywhere. I'm passionate about applying LLMs and ML to markets not just to predict, but to understand, explain, and act on financial complexity at scale.
03
Real Impact, Not Just Research
I'm not interested in AI for the sake of AI. Every system I build has a real human on the other end — an investor needing clarity, a patient needing guidance, a team needing efficiency. That responsibility sharpens everything I build.
